Growth

Seed

When we see a model in life, we generally tend to try, more or less consciously, to become more like it. Because we see something in this person that inspires us, or that feels so right, that we just want to take even just a piece of that for ourselves. Be it a value, a point of view, a character trait, many things can trigger this phenomenon.
Is it his unbreakable determination? The spark of wisdom is her eyes? His infinite kindness? Her unrelenting passion?

Sprout

Whatever might it be, there is something we want to take home, with us, within our very souls. Something that is so gorgeous that we feel our lack thereof. It’s not a feeling of inferiority, it’s not a feeling of not being enough.
It’s the realization that we could be more. That what this person has, that we don’t, would make us a better person. And this fragment of humility, recognizing that someone has something we don’t, it’s what will make us grow. Letting go of this ego that brings us down. Accepting the world the way it is, accepting who we are, and deciding to do something about it.

Flower

Ages after, after relentlessly finding new incredible people, after absorbing all their kindness and wisdom, all of their beautiful passion and determination, you end up struggling, with fewer and fewer bits of humanity left to gather. And at some point, confused and scared, you end up to the wall. Now it’s for you to become even better, to grow even more, radiating the beauty of a thousand friends, the wisdom of a thousand strangers, it’s your turn to create. It’s for you to continue to nurture these qualities, to find a way to be kinder even than the kindest person, to have more wisdom than even the oldest sage, and for your smile to warm a million hearts.

Pollination

When you contemplate this apparently insurmountable mountain, you are left confused, you are left clueless, wandering through the abyss of uncertainty. Of course, you learned to deal with it. Yet there is still concern to be had. The ominous feeling of forgetting something important.
You see the eyes of your young friend, watching you.
He’s looking at you with the same eyes you had when you started your adventure.

Now you know what to do.
