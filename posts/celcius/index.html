Celcius

Intro

First of all there is one difficult word in there : hearth which designates the floor of the fireplace. To appreciate this poem, I recommend you to look in the vague (outdoors if possible) and do not fix anything at all, just blurry vision, for like 30 seconds. No, not right now, at the end of the intro. Take a deep breath. Now look away and then don’t be distracted by anything.

Celcius

You know, they say it's warm.
And they say it warms your heart.
And even that some people smile.
But... My thermometer is broken. How can I find out ?

I tried to remember the warmth of the sun,
I think I caught sunburns.
I tried to remember the warmth of the hearth,
My eyes were lost in the ashes.

But nothing makes sense.

Because when I think about it,
And I find my tears flowing,
I know there is more.

You, who knows everything,
Could you tell me
The temperature of a hug ?

Lilli

Manifest

I know, the intro is weird, but I feel like if you just arrive here with all your thoughts and wanting to read quickly the new post of your acquaintance and then be done with it, you’re not really in the right mood see why just below.

Context

This poem was made during a fairly dark period of my life. A permanent struggle at the time. I was terribly lacking human contact, be it social or physical (hugs are drugs). I was sick, my mind was barely working. I wanted to share my confusion about how trivial things can become complicated. But in a cute way.

Comments

Once again, I imagine the character talking to be a little girl (and if it’s a mirror of myself, maybe it’s the little girl I was always forbidden to be). I like the idea of trying to show that things that we take for granted, can be much deeper than at first sight. And for this, what’s best than a fresh look on life: children. They look at everything and discover their emotions with the eye of discovery. And from time to time, they point something that we overlooked or had long forgotten. Even with that said, I find it kind of childish but not in the right way, I’m talking about the writer. It feels clumsy. Also I think there is potential for beautiful imagery here, but it could not go out of his brain. Let’s be kind to the sick me. I think that “temperature of a hug” is a beautiful image, and it love it. It’s saddening that I left it this way, it could have been so much more. Because I didn’t want to ruin this image before reading the poem, I called the poem Celcius. Oh by the way here is my inspiration:

It is from a mobile music game I love tremendously: Deemo. It made me discover the artist: V.K. (and Mili is fantastic as well) The song is indeed called 擁抱的溫度, or Temperature of a hug if, like me, you don’t read Chinese.
